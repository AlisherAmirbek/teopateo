# Quit-Smoking Companion Core Idea

## Core Idea

Build an iOS quit-smoking companion that helps someone move from "I should quit" to a concrete daily plan. The app should not feel like a generic habit tracker. It should act like a relapse-prevention system: identify when, where, and why the user smokes; prepare a quit plan; intervene during cravings; track progress; and help them recover after slips without restarting from zero.

The strongest version is:

> A personal quit coach for the exact moment you are about to smoke.

This matches standard cessation guidance: CDC emphasizes quit plans, counseling, and quit-smoking medicines; Smokefree.gov emphasizes cravings, triggers, support systems, and personalized quit plans.

Reference sources:

- CDC quit smoking
- CDC quit medicines
- Smokefree cravings and triggers
- Smokefree quit plan

## Business Model

Use a subscription model built around ongoing quit support instead of a fixed-duration program.

The free product should help the user understand the app, create a basic quit plan, track simple progress, and find trustworthy external support. The paid package should contain the strongest relapse-prevention tools, including full craving mode.

### Free Tier

- Basic onboarding.
- Basic quit plan.
- Daily check-in.
- Basic progress dashboard.
- Quitline and professional support prompts.
- High-level medication education.

### Paid Package

- Full craving emergency mode.
- Adaptive trigger and slip recovery flows.
- Personalized risky-window reminders.
- Pattern insights based on real craving and check-in history.
- AI coach for cravings, planning, reflection, and relapse prevention.
- Support-circle actions and preset messages.
- Motivation vault and richer quit history.

The subscription promise should be a continuing quit journey, not a pass/fail challenge. If the user smokes, the paid experience should adapt the plan and keep the journey alive rather than making the user feel like they wasted money.

## Main Features

### Personalized Onboarding

Capture the user's baseline and quitting context:

- Cigarettes per day
- Common smoking times
- Triggers
- Stress level
- Previous quit attempts
- Reasons for quitting
- Support people
- Quit date
- Interest in medication or professional support

### Quit Plan Builder

Help the user turn their quit intention into a practical plan:

- Quit date
- Taper or cold-turkey preference
- Trigger plan
- Craving plan
- Substitute activities
- Support contacts
- Risky situations
- "What I'll do instead" rules

### Craving Emergency Mode

Provide a one-tap "I want to smoke" button for the exact high-risk moment. This is the core paid feature because it is the app's strongest intervention surface. The flow should include:

- A short timer
- Breathing exercise
- Distraction task
- Motivational reason
- Support contact
- Quick trigger logging

### Trigger Mapping

Track cravings and smoking risk by:

- Time
- Location
- Mood
- Activity
- People
- Alcohol, coffee, meals
- Boredom
- Stress
- Social situations

### Pattern Insights

Turn logged behavior into useful observations, such as:

- "Your highest-risk window is 9:00-10:30 PM."
- "Coffee triggers 42% of cravings."
- "Work stress is your top relapse driver."

### Daily Check-In

Ask for a lightweight daily reflection:

- Mood
- Cravings
- Cigarettes avoided
- Slips
- Sleep
- Stress
- Confidence
- One focus for the day

### Streak And Progress Tracking

Show progress without making the app feel childish:

- Smoke-free days
- Cigarettes avoided
- Money saved
- Health milestones
- Personal goals

### Slip Recovery

If the user smokes, treat it as data instead of failure. The app should:

- Ask what happened
- Identify the trigger
- Update the trigger plan
- Keep the quit attempt alive
- Avoid resetting progress to zero unless the user explicitly chooses to restart

### Replacement Activity Library

Offer fast alternatives for cravings:

- Short walks
- Water
- Breathing
- Push-ups
- Chewing gum
- Shower
- Journaling
- Texting someone
- Games
- Timed distractions

### Support Circle

Help users get support without needing to compose a message under stress:

- Invite friends or family
- Send "I'm craving" alerts
- Share milestones
- Ask for help from preset messages

### Quitline And Professional Support

Surface trustworthy support paths:

- 1-800-QUIT-NOW in the US
- Doctor and pharmacist prompts
- Educational info about counseling
- Educational info about medication

### Medication Education

Explain nicotine replacement therapy and FDA-approved quit medicines at a high level. Use clear language that encourages the user to talk to a healthcare professional before making medication decisions.

### Motivation Vault

Store the user's personal reasons for quitting:

- Personal reasons
- Photos
- Voice notes
- Future-self letter
- Family goals
- Financial goals
- Health goals

### Smart Reminders

Support the quit plan with opt-in reminders:

- Morning plan
- Risky-time warnings
- Post-meal reminders
- Evening check-ins
- Medication or nicotine replacement therapy reminders if enabled

### Achievements Without Childishness

Recognize meaningful progress in a mature tone:

- First craving beaten
- First smoke-free day
- First week
- Money saved
- Repeated recovery after slips

### AI Coach

Provide conversational support for:

- Cravings
- Planning
- Reflection
- Pattern analysis, such as "Why do I keep smoking after work?"

### Private Journal

Let users record:

- Cravings
- Emotions
- Lessons
- Wins
- Relapse notes

### Dashboard

The dashboard should show:

- Today's risk level
- Current streak
- Cravings handled
- Money saved
- Next risky window
- One recommended action

### Community Option

Offer anonymous groups by:

- Quit date
- Age group
- Smoking pattern

### Content Library

Provide short, practical articles on:

- Withdrawal
- Cravings
- Triggers
- Social pressure
- Alcohol
- Stress
- Weight gain
- Sleep

### Privacy-First Mode

Make privacy a product feature:

- Local-only sensitive notes
- Minimal data collection
- No shame-based messaging

## MVP

The MVP should be narrow and strong:

- Onboarding
- Quit plan
- Paid craving emergency mode
- Daily check-in
- Progress dashboard
- Trigger logging
- Slip recovery
- Basic AI coach

That is enough to make the app useful from day one.

## Differentiator

The differentiator should be the craving moment. Most quit apps track progress after the fact. This one should help the user survive the exact 10 minutes where the quit attempt is at risk.
